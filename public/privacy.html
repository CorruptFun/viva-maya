Privacy Policy

Viva Maya · Last updated July 20, 2026

Viva Maya is a free match-3 game made by CorruptFun LLC. This policy explains, in plain language, what the game stores and why. The short version: we only keep what's needed to save your game progress, we don't sell or share it, and there are no ads or trackers.

What the game stores

  • Your game progress — levels completed, stars, best scores, in-game chips, lives, streaks, and your settings. This is saved on your own device.
  • If you sign in with Google (optional): your email address and a unique account ID. These are used only to save your game progress to the cloud so it syncs across your devices and survives clearing your browser or getting a new phone. Google shares your basic profile (name and email) with us when you choose to sign in; we do not receive your Google password.

What we don't do

  • No advertising and no third-party ad or analytics trackers.
  • We never sell, rent, or share your information with anyone.
  • We don't collect anything beyond what's listed above. In-game chips are for play only — they have no cash value and can't be purchased or cashed out.

Where it's stored

Game progress lives on your device. If you sign in, a copy is also stored in our database (hosted by Supabase), protected by security rules so that only you can read or change your own saved game — no other player can access it.

Your choices

  • Play without signing in — the game works fully offline, with progress kept only on your device.
  • Sign out any time from Settings → Cloud & Backup.
  • Delete your data — clear the game's data in your browser to remove the local copy, and email us to have your cloud save deleted.
  • Back up your progress to a file any time from Settings → Cloud & Backup.

Children & families

Viva Maya is a family-friendly game. We only ever store game progress and, if you sign in, the sign-in email described above — nothing more.

Changes

If we update this policy, we'll change the date at the top of this page.
